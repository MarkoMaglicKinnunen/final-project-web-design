History

Birmingham was founded in 1871 by the Elyton Land Company. The first settlers of Birmingham were Veterans of Andrew Jackson’s Army after they defeated the Creek Indians inhabiting the area. But in combination with the fact that there was not enough transportation to connect it to other settlements and that there was not enough fertile soil to grow mass amounts cotton, the settlement’s population grew slowly at first but then boomed after the Civil war due to the improvement of railroads and the discovery of vast amounts of minerals in the area. Because of this, Birmingham was beginning to aquire its nickname ‘The Magic City’.

Civil Rights

On September 15, 1963 a bomb exploded in the back stairway of the Sixteenth Street Baptist Church killing four innocent African American Girls named Addie Mae Collins, Cynthia Wesley, Carole Robertson, and Carol Denise McNair. The bombing also injured twenty other people. The perpetrators were Robert E. Chambliss, Bobby Frank Cherry, Herman Frank Cash, and Thomas E. Blanton, Jr. This act of terrorism left a scar on the history of Birmingham AL and was one of the many events that motivated the Civil Rights Movement to keep fighting for equality. Letter From The Birmingham Jail. Famous Civil Rights Activist, Martin Luther King Jr., was incarcerated at the Birmingham jail after staging Sit-ins. King was arrested because he violated a court injuction which forbade Civil Rights demonstrations in Birmingham. While King was in jail he wrote the famous Letter From the Birmingham Jail.

Facts

Birmingham is Alabama’s most populous city. Birmingham was founded at the crossing of two railroads. Birmingham is the national leader in urban green spaces. Birmingham’s largest employer is the healthcare industry. Birmingham hosts the oldest and largest veterans day parade The Birmingham Museum of Art houses 10,000 of Wedgwood which makes it the largest collection outside England. Birmingham is the youngest major city in Birmingham. Birmingham is located in one of the southernmost valleys in the Appalacian Mountain Chain.
